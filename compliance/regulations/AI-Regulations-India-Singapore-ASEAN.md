## India

### Overview
India has **no dedicated AI legislation** as of March 2026. The government relies on a mix of existing laws covering privacy, cybersecurity, and consumer protection, supplemented by voluntary ethical guidelines. India's approach emphasizes using AI to bridge socio-economic gaps while maintaining a relatively innovation-friendly regulatory posture.

### Key Frameworks

#### AI Governance Guidelines (2025)
Seven core principles:
1. Safety and reliability
2. Transparency and explainability
3. Fairness and non-discrimination
4. Accountability
5. Privacy and data protection
6. Human oversight
7. Sustainability and social benefit

Six key recommendations include education/skills training, policies supporting innovation, risk mitigation, and establishing an AI Governance Group supported by a Technology and Policy Expert Committee.

#### "Responsible AI for All" Strategy (NITI Aayog)
- National think tank strategy document
- Promotes ethical AI development
- Focus on using AI to address socio-economic inequality
- Principles: privacy, security, consent-based data use

#### AI Safety Institute
- Established to provide technical expertise on trust and safety
- Part of the broader governance recommendations

### Existing Laws Applying to AI

#### Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) 2023
- India's first comprehensive data protection law
- Enforcement scheduled to begin mid-to-late 2025
- Creates obligations for AI systems processing personal data:
  - Consent requirements
  - Purpose limitation
  - Data localization provisions for certain categories
  - Rights of data principals (access, correction, erasure)
- Automated decision-making provisions under development

#### Information Technology Act 2000 (and amendments)
- Governs cybersecurity, electronic commerce, and intermediary liability
- Section 79 intermediary safe harbor applies to AI-powered platforms with conditions
- IT Rules 2021 impose content moderation obligations on significant social media intermediaries

#### Consumer Protection Act 2019
- Prohibits unfair trade practices — applies to AI-driven commerce
- E-commerce rules apply to AI-powered retail platforms

#### Sector-Specific Regulation
- **Financial services:** RBI and SEBI oversight extends to AI in banking, insurance, and trading
- **Healthcare:** AI medical devices subject to CDSCO regulation
- **Telecommunications:** TRAI monitoring AI in telecom services

### India's Pattern
India follows "soft law for the whole ecosystem, hard law where the harm is evident" — voluntary guidelines broadly, with specific enforcement through existing sectoral laws and emerging privacy legislation.

### What This Means for Developers
1. **DPDPA compliance is the immediate priority** — enforce consent, purpose limitation, and data rights in AI systems
2. **No AI-specific compliance regime** — but existing laws apply broadly
3. **Content labeling and transparency** rules are being pressed by government
4. **AI governance guidelines are voluntary** but signal future direction
5. **Data localization** requirements may affect where AI training data and models can be stored/processed

## Singapore

### Overview
Singapore is the **governance and standards leader** in Asia-Pacific. It has chosen **not** to pursue comprehensive AI legislation, instead developing voluntary frameworks, testing toolkits, and sector-specific guidance that have become influential globally. The approach is practical and implementation-focused, making Singapore's frameworks popular as compliance benchmarks even outside the country.

### Key Frameworks

#### Model AI Governance Framework (2019, updated 2020)
- Voluntary guidelines for private sector AI deployment
- Covers transparency, accountability, fairness, and human oversight
- Widely adopted as a baseline governance standard

#### Model AI Governance Framework for Generative AI (2024)
- Extension of the original framework for generative AI
- Addresses foundation model risks, content provenance, and responsible deployment

#### AI Verify (Testing Framework)
- Singapore's flagship AI governance tool
- Voluntary certification and testing methodology
- Allows companies to validate AI systems against governance principles
- Open-source AI Verify Foundation supports community development
- Expanded Global AI Assurance Sandbox (2025) for real-world testing

#### National AI Strategy 2.0 (2023)
- Outlines ambition for trusted and responsible AI ecosystem
- S$100 million investment in broadband infrastructure for AI
- Building AI-fluent workforce across government and private sector
- Focus on economic uplift through AI adoption

### Sector-Specific Regulation

#### Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)
- **Veritas Toolkit:** Risk management and testing for AI in banking and finance
- **FEAT Principles:** Fairness, Ethics, Accountability, and Transparency for AI in financial services
- Binding within financial sector; influential beyond it

#### Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC)
- Designated as unified data protection supervisory authority for private entities
- Enforces Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) — applies to AI processing personal data
- Published guidance on AI and data protection

### ASEAN Guide on AI Governance and Ethics
- Singapore plays a leading role in ASEAN AI governance coordination
- Updated in 2025 to include generative AI
- Seven principles: transparency, fairness, security, reliability, human-centricity, privacy, accountability
- Non-binding but influential for regional alignment

### What This Means for Developers
1. **No mandatory AI-specific compliance** — but PDPA applies for personal data
2. **AI Verify provides a credible testing/certification path** — use it to demonstrate governance
3. **Financial services AI** is subject to MAS binding requirements (Veritas, FEAT)
4. **Singapore's frameworks are widely referenced** — compliance here carries credibility across APAC
5. **ASEAN-wide standards are emerging** — Singapore-aligned governance will ease regional expansion

## ASEAN Region Highlights

### Vietnam — First Southeast Asian AI Law
- **Digital Technology Industry Law** passed December 2025 — includes AI provisions
- Takes effect in phased approach starting **March 2026** over four years
- Risk-based framework influenced by both EU and South Korean approaches
- Regulatory infrastructure and authority appointments set for 2026

### Thailand
- **Draft AI regulation** being developed by ETDA (Electronic Transactions Development Agency)
- Public consultation completed 2025; revised draft pending
- Sector-based approach targeting finance, education, and healthcare

### Indonesia
- **AI framework** expected as Presidential regulations in early 2026
- Sector-specific focus; soft-law approach
- Building on existing data protection (PDP Law) and cybersecurity frameworks

### Philippines
- Plans to introduce AI regulatory framework during ASEAN chairmanship in 2026
- Multiple deepfake and AI governance bills under consideration
- Would be among first Southeast Asian nations with formal AI governance

### Malaysia
- Voluntary AI governance framework in place
- 13th Malaysia Plan (2026-2031) includes AI and digitalization priorities
- National AI Roadmap focuses on adoption and economic growth

## Regional Comparison

| Country | AI Law Status | Approach | Key Framework |
|---------|-------------|----------|--------------|
| **India** | No AI law; DPDPA enforcing | Voluntary + existing laws | AI Governance Guidelines 2025 |
| **Singapore** | No AI law | Voluntary frameworks + sector rules | AI Verify, Model AI Governance |
| **Vietnam** | AI law passed (Dec 2025) | Risk-based, phased rollout | Digital Technology Industry Law |
| **South Korea** | AI Basic Act (Jan 2026) | EU-style risk-based | AI Basic Act |
| **Japan** | AI Promotion Act (May 2025) | Innovation-first, principles | AI Guidelines for Business |
| **Thailand** | Draft regulation | Sector-based | ETDA draft principles |
| **Indonesia** | Expected early 2026 | Soft-law, sector-specific | Presidential regulations |
| **Philippines** | Proposed 2026 | Comprehensive framework planned | ASEAN chairmanship initiative |
