## Japan

### Overview
Japan has adopted a **light-touch, innovation-first approach** to AI governance. In May 2025, Japan's Parliament passed the **AI Promotion Act** â€” a principles-based law that encourages AI development and cooperation but lacks strict punitive measures. Japan has historically favored soft law (voluntary guidelines) over hard regulation, though it is moving toward more binding frameworks for high-risk scenarios.

### AI Promotion Act (May 2025)
- Approved by Japan's Parliament (Diet)
- Follows an "innovation-first" philosophy â€” lighter than the EU AI Act
- Principles-based: sets expectations for businesses to cooperate with AI governance principles
- Key principles include transparency, risk mitigation, and international cooperation
- Empowers the government to issue warnings and guidance
- **No strict penalties** â€” relies on reputational pressure, guidance, and cooperation duties
- Designed to push adoption while still shaping responsible behavior

### AI Guidelines for Business (Version 1.0)
- Voluntary guidelines published by the government
- Cover risk management, transparency, accountability, and human oversight
- Intended for developers and deployers of AI systems
- Not legally binding but influential for enterprise buyers and B2B relationships

### Hiroshima AI Process
- Japan-led G7 initiative for global AI governance, particularly for generative AI
- Established international guiding principles and a voluntary code of conduct for advanced AI developers
- Positions Japan as a leader in multilateral AI governance

### Existing Laws Applying to AI
- **Act on the Protection of Personal Information (APPI):** Data protection law applies to AI processing of personal data
- **Unfair Competition Prevention Act:** Applies to AI misuse in business
- **Product liability and consumer protection laws** apply to AI products
- Sector-specific regulations in finance, healthcare, and telecommunications

### AI Safety Institute
- Japan has established its own AI Safety Institute
- Conducts evaluations and testing of advanced AI models
- International collaboration with US and UK counterparts

### What This Means for Developers
1. Japan is innovation-friendly â€” no heavy compliance burden yet
2. Voluntary guidelines should be followed to build trust with Japanese enterprise clients
3. APPI applies if you process personal data of Japanese individuals
4. Monitor for potential shift toward mandatory rules for high-impact AI
5. Japan's AI Safety Institute may influence pre-deployment expectations for frontier models

## South Korea

### Overview
South Korea passed the **AI Basic Act** in December 2024, making it the **first comprehensive national AI law in Asia-Pacific** and the second globally after the EU. The law takes effect on **January 22, 2026**, establishing a risk-based regulatory framework with particular focus on high-impact AI systems.

### AI Basic Act â€” Key Provisions

#### Risk-Based Classification
The law distinguishes between:
- **High-Impact AI:** Systems used in healthcare, education, finance, employment, and essential services â€” subject to enhanced obligations
- **Other AI Systems:** Lower-risk applications with lighter requirements

#### Requirements for High-Impact AI
| Obligation | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Risk assessment** | Comprehensive assessment of impact on fundamental rights |
| **Human oversight** | Meaningful human monitoring and intervention capability at all times |
| **Disclosure** | Users must be informed when interacting with AI-generated content or AI decisions |
| **Documentation** | All AI risks and controls must be assessed, documented, and addressed |
| **Local representative** | Foreign AI companies must appoint a domestic agent for regulatory communications |
| **Bias monitoring** | Ongoing monitoring for discriminatory outcomes |

#### AI Safety Institute
- Mandated by the Act
- Conducts research and testing on AI safety
- Provides technical guidance to regulators and industry

#### AI Ethics
- Encourages establishment of ethics committees within organizations
- Emphasis on safety and ethical development throughout the AI lifecycle

### Integration with Existing Laws
The AI Basic Act works alongside:
- **Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA):** Requires user consent, limits data collection scope, applies to AI processing personal data
- **Network Act:** Governs digital services including AI-powered platforms
- **Product Liability Act:** Covers damages caused by AI products
- **Credit Information Act:** Applies to AI in financial services

### Enforcement
- The Act establishes institutional structures for oversight
- Detailed enforcement rules and penalties may follow after the January 2026 implementation
- One-year preparation period was provided (Dec 2024 â†’ Jan 2026)
- Additional sector-specific requirements expected to emerge

### What This Means for Developers
1. **January 2026 is the compliance deadline** â€” if you deploy AI in South Korea, act now
2. High-impact AI systems in healthcare, education, finance, and employment face strict requirements
3. **Foreign companies** must appoint a local representative if meeting certain thresholds
4. **User disclosure** is mandatory when AI generates content or makes decisions
5. **Risk assessments and documentation** are required â€” not optional
6. PIPA's personal data requirements layer on top of AI Basic Act obligations
7. South Korea's framework closely mirrors EU AI Act structure â€” EU compliance work can be leveraged
