## Overview

The UK has **no enacted comprehensive AI law** as of March 2026. It relies on a **sector-specific, principles-based approach** delivered through existing regulators (ICO, FCA, CMA, Ofcom, MHRA, etc.) rather than a single AI statute. A dedicated UK AI Bill is expected but unlikely before the second half of 2026. The Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 (DUA Act) has introduced significant AI-adjacent reforms to data protection, including relaxed automated decision-making rules and new deepfake offences.

## Regulatory Framework

### Five Core Principles (2023 AI White Paper)
Not currently legally binding, but regulators are expected to apply them sector-by-sector:
1. **Safety, Security & Robustness**
2. **Transparency & Explainability**
3. **Fairness**
4. **Accountability & Governance**
5. **Contestability & Redress**

The government intends to introduce a legal duty on regulators to give "due consideration" to these principles.

### No Formal AI Definition
The framework focuses on two AI characteristics rather than a fixed definition:
- **Adaptivity** â€” systems that learn patterns and make decisions not directly envisaged by programmers
- **Autonomy** â€” systems that take actions or make decisions without express human oversight

## Key Legislation and Developments

### Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 (DUA Act) â€” Major Reforms

| Provision | Effective Date | Impact |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| **Automated Decision-Making (ADM) reforms** | 5 Feb 2026 | Replaced old UK GDPR Art. 22; expands circumstances for automated decisions; reduces blanket prohibition |
| **Deepfake intimate images offence** | 6 Feb 2026 | Criminal offence for creating or requesting non-consensual intimate AI-generated images |
| **Scientific research broadened** | 5 Feb 2026 | Commercial research now qualifies; broad consent permitted for scientific research processing |
| **Copyright impact assessment** | Due by 18 Mar 2026 | Government must publish full report on AI and copyright, including training data and TDM options |

### AI Opportunities Action Plan (Jan 2025)
- 50 commitments for driving AI development and adoption
- 38 of 50 actions fulfilled by one-year review (Jan 2026)
- Includes dedicated AI growth zones, infrastructure investment, National Data Library
- Progress tracked via public dashboard

### UK AI Bill â€” Status
- Government announced first AI Bill **will not be introduced before second half of 2026**
- Expected to cover regulation of advanced/frontier AI models and AI copyright rules
- May be included in spring 2026 King's Speech
- A Private Members' Bill (Lord Holmes) was re-introduced in March 2025 but has no government backing
- That bill would create an "AI Authority" and mandate AI officers â€” but is unlikely to pass as-is

### AI Growth Lab (Proposed Oct 2025)
- Cross-economy sandboxes to safely test AI innovations under modified regulations
- Successful pilots could lead to permanent regulatory reforms
- Consultation closed January 2026; results pending

## Sector Regulators and AI

### Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)
- **AI and Biometrics Strategy** (June 2025): "Preventing Harm, Promoting Trust"
- Consulting on updated Automated Decision-Making guidance reflecting DUA Act
- Developing statutory code of practice on AI and ADM
- Scrutinizing ADM use in recruitment by major employers
- Securing assurances from foundation model developers on personal data in training
- Monitoring agentic AI systems with autonomous capabilities

### Financial Conduct Authority (FCA)
- AI oversight within existing financial services framework
- Focus on algorithmic trading, credit scoring, consumer protection

### Competition and Markets Authority (CMA)
- Foundation models market study
- Monitoring AI's impact on competition and market concentration

### Medicines & Healthcare Products Regulatory Agency (MHRA)
- Call for evidence on AI in healthcare regulation (closed Feb 2026)
- National Commission into the Regulation of AI in Healthcare established
- Recommendations expected 2026

### Ofgem (Energy)
- AI governance guidance for energy sector (May 2025)
- AI Regulatory Labs for industry testing against existing regulations
- Technical sandbox under development

### Civil Aviation Authority (CAA)
- AI-led safety reporting tools being implemented
- Budget for AI-powered regulatory capabilities in 2026/27

## AI Safety Institute (AISI)

- Established at Bletchley Park following the 2023 AI Safety Summit
- Currently operates through voluntary agreements with frontier AI developers
- Expected to become a **legal entity** with binding mandate
- Conducts pre-deployment evaluations of advanced AI models
- International collaboration with US AISI and other partners
- The government's shift toward statutory footing signals move from voluntary to mandatory safety testing

---

## Copyright and AI

This is the UK's most contested AI policy area:
- Government consultation on copyright and AI closed in early 2025
- Options included: TDM exception for all purposes, or transparency/licensing measures for AI training
- **DUA Act requires** government to publish impact assessment and full report by **18 March 2026**
- A Parliamentary Working Group on AI and copyright was established (June 2025)
- Government has said there is currently no "workable opt-out proposal on the table"
- The DUA Act controversially allows tech companies to use creative content for AI development unless rightsholders actively opt out
- Creative industries strongly oppose this approach

---

## Cyber Security and Resilience Bill

- Introduced to Parliament November 2025
- Updates the NIS Regulations framework (similar to EU NIS2)
- Expands scope to managed service providers, certain data centres, and large load controllers
- AI used in IT systems of relevant service providers must comply
- Expected to pass during 2026
