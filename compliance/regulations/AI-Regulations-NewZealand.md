# AI & LLM Software Development Regulations — New Zealand

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-13
**Tags:** #ai-regulation #new-zealand #privacy-act-2020 #software-development

---

## Overview

New Zealand takes a deliberately **light-touch, principles-based approach** to AI regulation. It was the last OECD country to publish a national AI strategy (July 2025) and has explicitly stated it will **not** introduce AI-specific legislation. Instead, existing technology-neutral laws — primarily the Privacy Act 2020 and consumer protection frameworks — are expected to adapt as needed. The government's focus is on AI adoption and economic productivity rather than prescriptive regulation.

---

## Key Policy Documents

### New Zealand's Strategy for Artificial Intelligence (July 2025)
- Title: "Investing with Confidence"
- Confirms the light-touch regulatory approach
- Focuses on AI adoption over AI development — leveraging global technology rather than building foundational models locally
- Aligns with OECD AI Principles (adopted by Cabinet June 2024)
- Estimates AI could add **NZD 76 billion** to GDP by 2038

### Responsible AI Guidance for Businesses (July 2025)
- Voluntary, practical guide for all NZ businesses regardless of size or industry
- Covers three areas: organizational considerations, AI governance, and AI system-specific risks
- Addresses cybersecurity, intellectual property, transparency, and risk management
- Government intends to build on this guidance over time

### Public Service AI Framework (January 2025)
- Guides responsible AI use across government agencies
- Led by the Government Chief Digital Officer (GCDO)

### Responsible AI Guidance for the Public Service: GenAI (February 2025)
- Updated guidance specifically for generative AI in government
- Covers governance, security, procurement, staff capabilities, misinformation
- Incorporates ethics, bias/fairness, accessibility (including Māori and Pacific peoples), and transparency

## Existing Laws Applying to AI

### Privacy Act 2020
- The primary legal safeguard for AI systems in New Zealand
- Principles-based and technology-neutral
- Governs collection, use, and disclosure of personal information
- Applies to AI systems processing personal data
- The Privacy Commissioner has joined international joint statements on trustworthy AI data governance

### Consumer Protection (Fair Trading Act 1986)
- Prohibits misleading and deceptive conduct — applies to AI-generated content and recommendations
- Commerce Commission oversight extends to AI-powered business practices

### Human Rights Act 1993
- Discrimination protections apply to AI-driven decisions in employment, services, and government
- Algorithmic bias can give rise to complaints

### Copyright Act 1994
- No specific AI provisions
- AI-generated content copyright status is unsettled
- Text and data mining for AI training is legally ambiguous

## OECD AI Principles Alignment

New Zealand formally adopted the OECD AI Principles in June 2024. These guide how existing legal frameworks apply to AI:

1. **Inclusive growth, sustainable development, and well-being**
2. **Human-centered values and fairness**
3. **Transparency and explainability**
4. **Robustness, security, and safety**
5. **Accountability**

The principles don't create additional regulatory overlay — they shape interpretation of existing law.

## Institutional Developments

### New Zealand Institute for Advanced Technology (Announced July 2025)
- NZD 231 million initiative (2025–2029), Auckland-based
- Incubated within MBIE, slated to become independent by July 2026
- Covers AI, quantum computing, and other advanced technologies
- Guided by PM's science advisory council

### AI Expert Advisory Panel
- Appointed by the GCDO on 30 June 2025
- Advises on responsible AI use in the public service

### Five Eyes Collaboration
- New Zealand participates in collective AI governance efforts as part of the Five Eyes nations
- Priorities include knowledge sharing and regulatory consistency with key trading partners

## Treaty of Waitangi Considerations

New Zealand's AI strategy uniquely incorporates **Treaty of Waitangi obligations**:
- AI guidance for the public service explicitly addresses Māori and Pacific peoples' perspectives
- Emphasis on fairness, equity, and accessibility in AI systems
- Ensures AI development respects indigenous rights and perspectives
- Inclusive governance approach is a distinguishing feature of NZ's AI framework

---

## Public Trust Deficit

- New Zealand ranks **third to last of 47 nations** in KPMG's global AI trust study
- Significant public concern about AI risks
- The Privacy Commissioner, academics, and civil society have called for stronger regulatory action
- The government has not yet responded publicly to these concerns

---

## What This Means for Developers

1. **No AI-specific compliance obligations** — but Privacy Act 2020 and consumer law apply in full
2. **Voluntary guidance is the primary framework** — aligning with the Responsible AI Guidance for Businesses demonstrates good practice
3. **Privacy by design** is expected — particularly for systems handling personal information
4. **Transparency matters** — if deploying AI in customer-facing roles, disclose its use
5. **Bias and fairness** — discrimination laws apply to AI-driven decisions, especially in employment and services
6. **Māori data sovereignty** considerations are relevant for public sector work and may influence private sector expectations
7. **Copyright is ambiguous** — exercise caution with NZ-sourced training data
8. **Watch for evolving guidance** — the government has signaled ongoing refinement of its approach

## Comparison with Australia

| Aspect | New Zealand | Australia |
|--------|------------|-----------|
| AI-specific legislation | None planned | None enacted; mandatory guardrails paused |
| Regulatory approach | Light-touch, voluntary guidance | Existing laws + voluntary guidance + AI Safety Institute |
| National AI Strategy | July 2025 | National AI Plan Dec 2025 |
| Safety Institute | No equivalent | Operational early 2026 |
| Key law | Privacy Act 2020 | Privacy Act 1988 (reforms Dec 2026) |
| Indigenous considerations | Treaty of Waitangi integrated | Less prominent in AI policy |
