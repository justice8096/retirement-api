## Overview

No African country has enacted standalone AI legislation as of March 2026, but the continent is experiencing rapid regulatory acceleration. Between 2024 and early 2026, at least 15 nations published national AI strategies or policies, the African Union adopted its Continental AI Strategy, and 49 countries endorsed the landmark Kigali Declaration on AI. Nigeria's National Digital Economy and E-Governance Bill — targeted for passage by end of March 2026 — may become the continent's first comprehensive binding AI law. Until hard law arrives, **data protection statutes serve as the primary enforceable framework** governing AI across Africa.

---

## AU Continental AI Strategy (Endorsed July 2024)

The African Union Executive Council endorsed the Continental AI Strategy on July 18–19, 2024 in Accra — Africa's first unified AI governance framework.

### Five Focus Areas
1. Harnessing AI benefits for development
2. Building capabilities and infrastructure
3. Minimizing risks and ensuring safety
4. Stimulating investment
5. Fostering continental and international cooperation

### Phased Rollout
- **Phase 1 (2025–2026):** Establish governance structures, national AI strategies, resource mobilization
- **Phase 2 (from 2028):** Launch large-scale continental AI initiatives

### Key 2025 Milestones
- **Kigali Global AI Summit (April 2025):** Africa Declaration on AI endorsed by 49 countries; announced $60B Africa AI Fund; established Africa AI Council
- **May 2025:** AU High-Level Policy Dialogue — heads of state declared AI a strategic continental priority
- **Feb 2026:** African Development Bank and UNDP launched AI 10 Billion Initiative at Nairobi AI Forum

### Regional Bodies
- **ECOWAS:** Finalizing Draft Directive on Electronic Communications; parliament called for Community Act on AI
- **EAC:** Validated Data Governance Policy Framework (Oct 2024)
- **SADC:** Windhoek Statement on AI (2022) committing to national AI policy development
- **Pan-African Parliament:** Called for development of continental model law on AI

## Country-by-Country Breakdown

### Nigeria — Most Advanced Legislative Pipeline

**Status:** Multiple AI bills before National Assembly; passage of Digital Economy Bill targeted March 2026

#### Key Legislation
- **Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023:** Section 37 grants right not to be subject to solely automated decisions; NDPC levied ₦766.2M fine on Multichoice and $220M penalty on Meta
- **General Application and Implementation Directive (GAID) — Effective Sept 2025:** Requires AI deployers to conduct DPIAs, implement privacy by design, respect automated decision-making rights
- **National Digital Economy and E-Governance Bill:** Risk-based AI classification, mandatory algorithmic transparency, regulatory sandboxes, power to accredit AI auditors — potentially first binding AI law in Africa
- **Digital Sovereignty, AI Governance Bill:** Digital Services Tax, 30% local content requirement for AI research on Nigerian data
- **National AI Commission Bill:** Would create dedicated AI regulatory agency

#### National AI Strategy (Sept 2025)
- Developed by NITDA's National Centre for AI and Robotics (NCAIR)
- NCAIR developing **N-ATLAS** — open-source multilingual LLM for Yoruba, Hausa, Igbo, and Nigerian-accented English

---

### South Africa — Sector-Specific Multi-Regulator Model

**Status:** Draft National AI Policy Framework cleared government review; 60-day public consultation expected March 2026

#### AI Policy Framework (Draft, Oct 2024)
Five pillars: capacity development, responsible governance, ethical/inclusive AI, cultural preservation, human-centered deployment

#### POPIA (Effective July 2020)
- **Section 71:** Prohibits decisions with legal consequences based solely on automated profiling
- Requires disclosure of underlying logic in automated decisions
- Enforced by the Information Regulator

#### Key Institutions
- **AI Institute of South Africa (AIISA)** — established Nov 2022
- **Centre for Artificial Intelligence Research (CAIR)**
- **African Hub on AI Safety, Peace and Security** — launched at University of Cape Town, late 2025
- Financial Sector Conduct Authority published joint AI report (Nov 2025) urging explainability standards

#### Timeline
- Policy finalization targeted for 2026/2027 financial year
- No standalone AI legislation expected in the near term

### Kenya — Positioning as Africa's AI Hub

**Status:** National AI Strategy launched; draft AI Code of Practice unfinalized

#### National AI Strategy 2025–2030 (Launched March 2025)
- Positions Kenya as Africa's AI hub
- Focus on agriculture, healthcare, education, and financial services

#### Data Protection Act 2019
- Automated decision-making protections enforced by Office of the Data Protection Commissioner
- Suspended Worldcoin for 12 months (2023); fined digital lenders for unlawful data practices
- First African country to begin EU adequacy dialogue (May 2024)

#### Kenya Bureau of Standards
- Draft AI Code of Practice (DKS 3007) published April 2024 — remains unfinalized

---

### Rwanda — First African Country with National AI Policy

**Status:** National AI Policy adopted April 2023

#### National AI Policy
- Envisions Rwanda as "Africa's AI Lab and Responsible AI Champion"
- Proposes Responsible AI Office within Ministry of ICT
- Covers AI ethics across the full lifecycle

#### Key Institutions
- **Centre for the Fourth Industrial Revolution (C4IR)** — WEF partner hub
- $7.5M MoU with Gates Foundation for AI Scaling Hub
- Regulatory authority RURA has developed ethical AI guidelines

---

### Egypt — Most Comprehensive North African Framework

**Status:** Draft AI Law under development; expanded AI Council (Jan 2026)

#### National AI Strategy (2nd Edition, 2025–2030)
- Target: 30,000 AI specialists, 250 AI-driven companies, ICT GDP contribution to 7.7% by 2030
- Egyptian Charter for Responsible AI (2023) — voluntary ethical principles

#### Draft AI Law (In Development)
- Risk-based classification
- Mandatory human oversight
- Requirement that foreign companies train models on local data

#### Personal Data Protection Law
- Executive Regulations issued November 1, 2025 (after 5-year delay)
- **Full enforcement expected November 1, 2026**

#### National Council for AI
- Expanded January 2026 to cover quantum computing and emerging technologies

---

### Ethiopia — State-Led Centralized Approach

**Status:** National AI Policy approved June 2024; dedicated AI Institute established

#### Ethiopian AI Institute (EAII)
- Autonomous federal body reporting directly to Prime Minister
- 42% budget increase in 2025 (~1.13 billion Birr)
- Authority to certify imported AI technologies

#### Personal Data Protection Proclamation No. 1321/2024
- Enacted July 2024; mirrors GDPR
- Explicitly grants right to challenge AI-only decisions in credit, hiring, and insurance

## Data Protection as De Facto AI Regulation

Across Africa, data protection laws provide the most immediately enforceable AI governance:
- **36 of 54 African countries** now have data protection legislation
- Automated decision-making provisions exist in Nigeria, South Africa, Kenya, Ethiopia, Ghana, Botswana, and others
- 2025 was described as "The Year of the Teeth" for African data protection enforcement — regulators issued record fines
- The AU's Malabo Convention on Cybersecurity and Data Protection provides a continental framework, though ratification remains limited

## Africa's Distinctive AI Governance Themes

### Digital and Linguistic Sovereignty
- Nigeria's N-ATLAS multilingual LLM
- Morocco's Mistral AI partnership for Arabic and Amazigh models
- Cameroon's "GPT Cameroon" initiative
- Emphasis on African open datasets and open AI models (Kigali Declaration)

### Infrastructure Gap
- Africa holds only **1% of global AI compute capacity** and **3% of global AI talent**
- 83% of startup funding concentrated in Nigeria, South Africa, Kenya, and Egypt
- Chinese investment reshaping the landscape — Huawei announced $430M for African data centers
- DeepSeek adoption in Africa runs 2–4x higher than other regions

### AI Safety
- African Hub on AI Safety, Peace and Security at UCT (late 2025)
- No African member of International Network of AI Safety Institutes as of March 2026
- Proposals for annual AU-level AI Safety Forum and early warning systems with multilingual benchmarks

## What This Means for Developers

1. **No binding AI-specific laws yet** — but data protection laws apply now (NDPA, POPIA, Kenya DPA)
2. **Nigeria's Digital Economy Bill is imminent** — if you serve Nigerian users, prepare for risk-based AI classification
3. **South Africa's POPIA Section 71** prohibits automated profiling decisions with legal effects — comply now
4. **Automated decision-making rights** exist in multiple countries — build opt-out and human review mechanisms
5. **Local content and data sovereignty requirements** are emerging — Nigeria's proposed 30% local content rule, Egypt's local data training requirement
6. **Multilingual AI** is a growing regulatory and market expectation — support African languages where possible
7. **The AU Continental AI Strategy** provides the directional framework — alignment with OECD AI Principles and AU pillars demonstrates good governance
8. **Watch Nigeria and South Africa** — their regulatory choices will influence the continent
