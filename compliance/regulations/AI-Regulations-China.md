## Overview

China is the **most actively enforced** AI regulatory environment outside the EU. It was the first country with binding regulations for generative AI (August 2023) and has built a layered system of algorithm registration, content labeling, generative AI service rules, and ethics reviews. Enforcement is already happening â€” fines, app takedowns, and mandatory compliance are real. In October 2025, the amended Cybersecurity Law brought AI into national law for the first time, effective January 1, 2026. The National People's Congress has prioritized comprehensive AI legislative research for 2026, signaling a formal AI law may follow in 2027-2028.

## Enacted Regulations (Already in Force)

### Administrative Provisions on Recommendation Algorithms (Mar 2022)
- Regulates algorithm-based content recommendation services
- Mandatory algorithm filing with the Cyberspace Administration of China (CAC)
- Transparency requirements for how algorithms influence content delivery

### Administrative Provisions on Deep Synthesis (Jan 2023)
- Governs deep synthesis technologies (deepfakes, voice cloning, face swapping)
- Mandatory labeling of deep-synthesized content
- Registration and security assessment requirements

### Interim Measures for Generative AI Services (Aug 15, 2023)
- **First binding generative AI regulation globally**
- Applies to providers offering generative AI services to the public in China
- Key requirements:
  - Content must be lawful, truthful, and not harmful to national security
  - AI-generated content must be labeled
  - Mandatory algorithm filing with the CAC
  - LLMs with public opinion attributes must undergo security assessments
  - Mandatory LLM filing (separate from algorithm filing)
  - Providers must implement content moderation and user complaint mechanisms
- **Exemption:** R&D and internal use of generative AI are excluded from compliance requirements
- Hundreds of generative AI platforms registered (including DeepSeek, Baidu's Ernie Bot)

### Measures for Labeling AI-Generated Content (Sept 1, 2025)
- **Explicit labels:** Visible to users â€” required on text, audio, images, videos, and virtual scenes
- **Implicit labels:** Embedded in metadata â€” include content attributes, provider name, content number
- Content platforms must verify implicit labels and add explicit labels if AI-generated content is detected
- User agreements must outline labeling obligations
- Users may request content without explicit labels, but implicit labels must remain; providers must retain logs for 6+ months

### National Security Standards (Nov 1, 2025)
Three national standards for generative AI security:
1. **Data Annotation Security Specification** â€” security for training data labeling
2. **Pre-training and Fine-tuning Data Security Specification** â€” dataset security requirements
3. **Basic Security Requirements for GenAI Services** â€” operational security for AI services

### Amended Cybersecurity Law (Jan 1, 2026)
- First time AI is referenced in China's national law
- Mandates AI ethics compliance, risk monitoring, and safety assessment
- Supports R&D of algorithms and construction of training data resources/computing infrastructure
- Signals comprehensive AI regulation is coming

### Livestreaming E-Commerce AI Rules (Feb 1, 2026)
- AI-generated human images/videos in livestream commerce must be clearly labeled and continuously disclosed
- Operators using AI-generated characters bear legal liability for violations

## Enforcement in Practice

China actively enforces its AI regulations:
- **"Clear and Bright" Campaign (April-June 2025):** Nationwide crackdown on AI misuse â€” over 3,500 AI products taken down, 960,000+ pieces of illegal content scrubbed, 3,700+ accounts penalized
- **Shanghai CAC (June 2025):** AI chatbot apps summoned for generating vulgar content; ordered to improve content review
- **Chongqing CAC (July 2024):** AI services suspended for providing generative AI without completing security assessments and LLM filings
- As of October 2025, thousands of algorithm filings have been approved

## Upcoming Legislation

### Comprehensive AI Law (In Research Phase)
- National People's Congress prioritized AI legislative research for 2026
- Proposal introduced June 2025 by NPC representatives â€” would:
  - Support AI innovation
  - Create risk classification and regulation system
  - Establish ethical impact assessment framework
  - Clarify legal obligations across AI lifecycle
  - Promote international cooperation
- **Timeline:** Stakeholder consultations and drafting expected 2026-2027; potential enactment 2027-2028
- Government aims for 50+ AI sector standards by 2026

### Draft Rules for Human-Like Interactive AI (Dec 2025)
- Targets empathetic and companion AI services
- Establishes requirements for privacy protection, ethical constraints, and safety safeguards

## AI Ethics Review

### Ethics Review Measures (Dec 2023)
- Mandatory ethical review for AI R&D activities in biological and medical fields
- Expanded in August 2025 with additional requirements

### Patent and IP Rules (Jan 1, 2026)
- Inventors must be natural persons â€” AI cannot be designated as an inventor
- Patent specifications for AI inventions must describe model architectures, training processes, and key parameters
- AI-generated voices recognized as protected personality rights (Supreme Court, May 2025)

## Key Compliance Requirements for Developers

### If You Offer Generative AI Services in China:
1. **Register algorithms** with the CAC (mandatory)
2. **File LLMs** separately if they have public opinion or social mobilization capabilities
3. **Complete security assessments** before offering public services
4. **Implement content labeling** â€” both explicit (visible) and implicit (metadata)
5. **Moderate content** â€” must be lawful and not harmful to national security, social stability
6. **Handle user complaints** â€” established complaint/reporting mechanisms required
7. **Maintain logs** â€” retain relevant records for minimum 6 months
8. **Comply with data security standards** â€” training data, annotation, and pre-training/fine-tuning security

### Penalties
- Administrative fines imposed on companies and responsible individuals
- Business suspension orders
- Criminal liability for the most serious violations
- App/service takedowns and account closures
